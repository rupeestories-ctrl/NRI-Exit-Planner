💸 Where People Lose Money
The avoidable mistakes — read this before you start
▾
₹Breaking FDs early without checking the premature penalty — often 0.5–1% interest loss, sometimes more. Run the numbers first.
🏠Paying full TDS on property without planning a refund — TDS is on sale value, not profit. The excess sits with the govt until you file an ITR to recover it. Many NRIs never file.
📈Selling MF without cost basis — no purchase records = cannot claim correct gains = potential over-taxation or ITR errors. Get your CAMS history first.
📅Not timing NRO transfers across financial years — the $1M limit is per April–March year per person. Moving ₹2Cr in March and April = two separate limits. Moving it all in March = one.
📋Wiring NRO funds without Form 15CA — the bank will usually block or delay the transfer. Have forms ready before you visit.
🔄Losing money silently on the FX conversion — banks typically charge a 0.5–2% spread on the exchange rate. On ₹1 Cr, that is ₹50K–₹2L gone before it even reaches your US account. Compare rates across banks or use a wire service before committing.

Tax & Compliance Gaps
Silent penalties that compound. Check honestly.
✓
❗ MF or stocks entered but no cost basis confirmation
Without original purchase price you cannot compute capital gains, file accurately, or determine the correct tax. Get purchase history from CAMS, KFintech, or your broker before selling.
Impact: over-taxation or ITR errors; may delay CA sign-off
❗ Indian ITR not filed for years with India income
Banks request ITR acknowledgments before large repatriations — this is a transfer readiness issue, not just a compliance one. File all pending ITRs before initiating. Your CA can help prioritise which years need filing first.
Impact: bank may refuse to process large outward remittances
❗ Unpaid Indian taxes — advance tax, TDS shortfall, or outstanding dues
Distinct from missing ITRs: this is about tax dues already owed but not paid. Outstanding amounts attract 1–1.5% interest per month and can trigger a block on the remittance itself. Your CA must confirm a clean tax position before processing.
Impact: remittance blocked; monthly interest compounding until resolved
❗ FBAR (FinCEN 114) not filed in the US
Required if total foreign accounts exceeded $10,000 at any point in the year — NRE, NRO, MF folios, demat all count. $10,000 penalty per violation (non-willful). Check every prior year.
Impact: $10,000+ penalty per year per account; criminal risk if wilful
❗ Form 8938 (FATCA) not filed for high-balance years
Required when foreign financial assets exceed $50K end of year or $75K at any point (single filers). Filed with federal return. $10,000 penalty for failure.
Impact: $10,000+ penalty; flags your account with IRS
Estate & Access Gaps
Invisible until there is a crisis.
❗ No nominee on some or all accounts
Without nominees, funds get locked in succession proceedings across two countries. Add nominees to every bank account, MF folio, and demat account.
Impact: estate proceedings across two countries; months or years of delays
❗ Spouse cannot access accounts or India property independently
If something happened tonight, could your spouse handle the repatriation process alone? Power of Attorney for India + a shared account document is the practical fix.
❗ Cannot clearly explain the source of some funds (old transfers, gifts, cash deposits)
Banks and your CA may delay or decline to process the remittance if the source of funds is unclear. This comes up more than people expect — old parental transfers, cash FDs, property sale proceeds from years ago. Document the origin of each major balance before starting.

Phase 1
Establish Your New Status
Legal & tax identity first — everything flows from this
▾
💡 Why this comes first. Your repatriation rights, tax obligations, and which accounts you can hold all depend on your FEMA residential status. Establish this before anything else.
Understand Your Status
Confirm you are NRI under FEMA (<182 days in India in the FY)
This determines repatriation rights — not your visa status or citizenship. Your CA can confirm.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
Notify all Indian banks of NRI status in writing
Each bank needs a written notification. Required before repatriation. Resident accounts are then redesignated to NRO by the bank.
⚡ Do this first
Ensure all resident savings accounts are redesignated to NRO
Resident rupee accounts are redesignated to NRO when a resident becomes non-resident. Continuing to operate one without redesignation is a compliance gap.
⚡ Do this first
Professional Setup

Ask specifically for experience with Form 15CA/15CB and NRI ITR filings. Not a general practice CA — the 15CB process and NRI tax rules are specialised.

Run a full inventory of every India financial account and asset
NRE/NRO accounts, FDs, MF folios (CAMS CAS), demat, property, EPF (EPFO portal via UAN), PPF, LIC policies, NPS. You cannot move what you do not know exists.

Phase 2
Sort Your Bank Accounts
NRE = free and direct. NRO = Form 15CA, and 15CB when required.
▾
💡 NRE is the easiest path. Wire NRE funds directly — no limit, no Indian tax, no forms. For NRO: most remittances require Form 15CA. Form 15CB is additionally required when the remittance is taxable and exceeds ₹5 lakh in a financial year, unless an exemption or AO certificate applies.
NRE — wire directly
Identify all NRE savings accounts and FDs
NRE holds funds that originated abroad. Interest on NRE accounts is exempt from Indian income tax.
🏦 Bank action
Wire NRE balance directly to your US bank — no paperwork required
No limit, no TDS, no Form 15CA. Standard SWIFT wire from NRE account to US bank. Do this before anything else.
🏦 Bank action
Calculate premature FD penalty before breaking NRE FDs early
Typically 0.5–1% interest penalty for premature closure. Weigh against benefit of moving the funds now.
☑️ One-time
NRO — Form 15CA required; 15CB when applicable
Understand the $1M annual NRO repatriation limit
FEMA: up to USD $1M from NRO per financial year (April–March) per person. A couple can use both limits — $2M/year combined. Spread large amounts across financial years if needed.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
Compute all India income and clear any pending tax with your CA
Outstanding tax dues block remittance. Your CA must confirm all taxes are paid before certifying 15CB or processing the outward remittance.
📞 Tax matter
CA issues Form 15CB where the remittance is taxable and exceeds ₹5L
Your CA certifies taxes are paid on the funds being remitted. This is a CA-issued certificate — cannot be self-filed. Usually handled on a per-remittance basis, depending on the bank’s process.
📞 Tax matter
File Form 15CA on incometax.gov.in before the remittance
Your self-declaration filed online. Required for most NRO foreign remittances. File after 15CB is ready if 15CB is applicable.
📞 Tax matter
Submit bank’s A2 form (Purpose of Remittance) with documents
Banks require their own A2 form plus 15CA and 15CB (where applicable). Ask your specific bank for their current checklist before visiting — requirements vary.
🏦 Bank action
Collect Form 16A (TDS certificate) for all TDS deducted on NRO interest
Banks auto-deduct 30% TDS on NRO interest. Form 16A is needed to claim credit in your Indian ITR and US Foreign Tax Credit filing.
📞 Tax matter
Account Hygiene
Formally close dormant accounts you no longer need
Do not just stop using them. Formally close — dormant NRO accounts create ongoing compliance and reporting obligations.
☑️ One-time
Add or update nominees on all active accounts
Add nominees to every bank account, MF folio, and demat. Without nominees, funds get caught in succession proceedings across two countries.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA

Phase 3
Liquidate Investments
MF, stocks, FDs, PPF — sell, pay tax, move to NRO, remit
▾
💡 The flow for all investments is the same: Sell → TDS deducted at source → Proceeds hit NRO → 15CA (+ 15CB if applicable) → Wire to US. Know the TDS rate and have cost basis before selling.
Mutual Funds
Get Consolidated Account Statement from CAMS or KFintech
Request CAS using your PAN and email. Shows every folio across all AMCs in one document. Do this before anything else.
☑️ One-time
Collect purchase history and cost basis per folio
Without original purchase price you cannot compute capital gains accurately or claim the correct tax treatment. Get from CAMS, KFintech, or AMC statements before selling.
⚡ Do this first
Update KYC to NRI status with each AMC before redeeming
Resident KYC may block redemption at some AMCs. Update to NRI KYC first. Allow a few days per AMC.
☑️ One-time
Redeem — TDS usually deducted by AMC, proceeds credited to NRO
TDS treatment varies by fund type and holding period. Equity: typically 15% on STCG (<1yr), 12.5% on LTCG (>1yr, gains above ₹1.25L). Debt: 30% flat for NRIs. Confirm with AMC before large redemptions. Then use 15CA process to remit from NRO.
📞 Tax matter
Stocks & Demat
Update broker/demat account to NRI status before selling
Some brokers require a fresh NRI-linked demat account. Check with your broker. Also collect broker statements with cost basis per holding.
☑️ One-time
Sell stocks — proceeds to NRO — then remit via 15CA process
Equity: 15% STCG (<1yr), 12.5% LTCG (>1yr above ₹1.25L). Do not assume TDS certificates from broker will auto-populate your ITR — verify each one.
📞 Tax matter
Fixed Deposits
Let FDs mature or calculate premature closure penalty first
NRO FD interest attracts 30% TDS. On maturity, principal + post-TDS interest credited to NRO. Calculate whether breaking early makes financial sense.
📞 Tax matter
PPF (Public Provident Fund)
Stop any ongoing PPF contributions
NRIs cannot continue fresh PPF contributions after becoming non-resident. Existing accounts can generally be held until maturity, but extension with fresh deposits after maturity is not allowed.
⚡ Do this first
Hold PPF to maturity — then withdraw tax-free via NRO
Existing PPF can be held to its 15-year maturity. Full corpus is tax-free on withdrawal, then remit from NRO via standard 15CA process.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
File ITR for year of sale to claim any excess TDS refund
If TDS deducted (especially 30% on debt MF or NRO interest) exceeds actual tax liability, file ITR to recover the overpaid amount to your NRO account.
📞 Tax matter

Phase 4
Deal with Real Estate
TDS, capital gains, repatriation limits — the most complex piece
▾
💡 Real estate is the hardest asset to move. High TDS, multiple approvals, and category-specific repatriation rules. The buyer is legally required to deduct TDS from your sale amount. Start early and use a CA who specialises in NRI property sales.
Before You Sell
Gather all original purchase documents for cost basis
Sale deed, registration, builder agreement, payment receipts, home loan closure certificate. Needed to calculate actual capital gains and apply for lower TDS if eligible.
☑️ One-time
Brief the buyer: they must deduct TDS from sale price
LTCG (>2 years): effective TDS typically ~23% including surcharge. STCG (<2 years): ~35% effective. TDS is on the sale value, not the profit — so a ₹1Cr sale can mean ₹23L+ deducted upfront even if your actual gains are lower. The excess is refunded later via ITR filing. This is a legal obligation on the buyer, not optional.
⚡ Do this first
Apply for lower TDS certificate under Section 195(3) if eligible
If actual capital gains tax is lower than the applicable TDS rate, your CA can apply to the Income Tax Department. File early — typically takes 30–60 days.
📞 Tax matter
After Sale
Collect Form 16A (TDS certificate) from the buyer
Confirms TDS deducted and deposited with government. Use to claim credit in Indian ITR and recover excess TDS.
📞 Tax matter
File ITR for year of sale and claim excess TDS refund
Capital gains must be declared in Indian ITR even as an NRI. If TDS exceeded actual tax liability, the refund goes to your NRO account.
📞 Tax matter
Repatriation
Confirm repatriation eligibility with CA and bank before assuming full repatriability
Residential property repatriation has category-specific limits and conditions depending on how the property was acquired. Get CA and bank confirmation for your specific situation.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
Submit 15CA + 15CB (where applicable) with full property docs — allow 2–4 weeks
Docs needed: sale deed copy, capital gains computation, ITR acknowledgment, Form 16A, KYC. Banks take longer for large property remittances.

Phase 5
Insurance & Retirement Funds
LIC, EPF, NPS, Gratuity — do not leave money behind
▾
💡 These are often forgotten. EPF from a job you left years ago. LIC your parents took in your name. Dormant NPS account. Run a full inventory before deciding anything.
EPF
Locate all EPF accounts via EPFO portal (UAN login)
Check all past employers. Inactive EPF still earns interest (8.25% currently) but verify current EPFO policy on non-contributing accounts before deciding to withdraw.
☑️ One-time
Confirm tax position with CA before EPF withdrawal
5+ years service usually means cleaner tax treatment, but actual tax and TDS on EPF withdrawal can vary — especially for non-residents. PAN status and applicable treaty angles also matter. Check before withdrawing.
📞 Tax matter
Claim pending gratuity from last Indian employer
5+ years service = entitled to gratuity. Gratuity up to ₹20L is tax-free. Contact HR of last employer if not already paid.
☑️ One-time
NPS
Decide: premature NPS exit vs hold to 60
Premature exit: 80% must buy annuity in India (only 20% lump sum accessible). At 60: 60% lump sum (tax-free), 40% mandatory annuity. Model both with your CA before deciding.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
LIC & Insurance
Inventory all LIC, ULIP, and endowment policies
Check surrender value vs maturity value and remaining premiums. For policies in the final 2–3 years, waiting to maturity often beats surrendering at a penalty.
☑️ One-time
Surrender LIC — proceeds to NRO — then remit via 15CA process
Generally tax-free under Section 10(10D) if premium <10% of sum assured. Stop paying premiums on policies you plan to exit. Consider the paid-up option if not surrendering.
🏦 Bank action

Phase 6
India + US Tax Filings
Stay compliant on both sides — missing filings block transfers
▾
💡 Both countries need attention. India wants your ITR before releasing large remittances. The US wants FBAR and FATCA filings. Missing either creates penalties that compound.
India ITR
File ITR for all years you had India income above ₹2.5L
Rental income, capital gains, NRO interest, dividends all count. NRE interest is exempt. File pending years before initiating large transfers — banks will ask for acknowledgments.
📞 Tax matter
Claim DTAA relief — attach Form 67 with Indian ITR
The India–US DTAA helps avoid double taxation through credits — not by eliminating tax on both sides. You often pay tax in both countries, then claim credit to offset. File Form 67 with Indian ITR. Your India CA and US CPA need to coordinate.
📞 Tax matter
Claim excess TDS refunds via ITR
TDS deducted at 30% may exceed actual tax liability, especially for smaller gains or exempt income. File ITR to recover the difference to your NRO account.
📞 Tax matter
US Filings
File FBAR (FinCEN 114) for all years with foreign accounts >$10K
NRE, NRO, MF folios, demat all count toward the $10K threshold. Deadline April 15 (auto-extended to Oct 15). Check every prior year — $10K penalty per violation (non-willful).
⚡ Do this first
File Form 8938 (FATCA) if foreign assets exceeded $50K
Filed with federal return. $50K end-of-year or $75K at any point (single filers). $10K penalty for failure. Your US CPA should check prior years as part of onboarding.
⚡ Do this first
Claim Foreign Tax Credit on IRS Form 1116
Indian taxes paid (TDS on NRO interest, capital gains tax) reduce US tax liability dollar for dollar. Your US CPA files this. Prevents true double taxation under the DTAA.
📞 Tax matter

Phase 7
Execute the Transfers
Wire it home — the final mechanics
▾
💡 This is the payoff phase. Phases 1–6 done correctly makes this straightforward. Most blocked transfers happen because paperwork was not ready — not because the money is hard to move.
Execution Order
Build your full document packet before going to the bank
Form 15CA + 15CB (where applicable) + source of funds proof + PAN + passport + ITR acknowledgment + KYC documents. Incomplete submissions reset the processing timeline.
☑️ One-time
Wire NRE funds first — no forms, no limit, no TDS
Always process NRE first. Initiate a SWIFT wire from NRE to your US bank. Nothing else needed.
🏦 Bank action
Spread NRO transfers across financial years if above $1M per person
$1M per person per April–March year from NRO. Use both spouses’ limits simultaneously if applicable. Spread large amounts across years.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
Only use SWIFT wire transfers — no exceptions
Informal channels (hawala or otherwise) are FEMA violations with criminal penalties. Use only official SWIFT from your Indian bank. Keep every SWIFT confirmation.
⚡ Do this first
After Transfer
Retain all remittance records for 7 years
SWIFT confirmations, 15CA/15CB copies, A2 forms, TDS certificates. Required for both Indian and US tax purposes.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
Brief your US CPA on the source of each transfer before they file
Investment proceeds, rental income, and inherited funds have different US tax treatment. Your CPA needs the source and nature of each transfer — not just the amount.

Bank Accounts
NRE Account Non-Resident External
Holds money from abroad. Interest tax-free in India. Full balance wired to US with no limit, no tax, no paperwork. Wire NRE first, always.
NRO Account Non-Resident Ordinary
India-sourced income. $1M per person per FY repatriation limit. Needs Form 15CA (always) and 15CB (when taxable >₹5L). 30% TDS on interest.
Resident Savings Account
Must be redesignated to NRO when you become NRI. Operating one as an NRI is a FEMA compliance gap.
Forms & Documents
Form 15CA
Self-declaration to Indian IT Dept. Filed online on incometax.gov.in before wire. Required for most NRO foreign remittances.
Form 15CB
CA certificate that taxes are paid. Needed when taxable remittance >₹5L in a FY. CA-issued, cannot be self-filed.
Form A2
Your bank’s internal purpose-of-remittance form. Submitted with 15CA/15CB. Every bank has their own version.
Form 16A
TDS certificate from bank/AMC/broker/buyer. Use this to claim TDS as credit in your Indian ITR.
FBAR  FinCEN 114
US filing when foreign accounts exceeded $10,000 at any point in the year. NRE, NRO, MF folios, demat all count. $10,000+ penalty per violation.
Form 8938  FATCA
IRS form for foreign assets above $50K year-end or $75K at any point. Filed with federal return. $10,000 penalty.
Form 3520
IRS form for gifts/inheritances from foreign persons above $100,000. Penalty: 25% of unreported amount. Commonly missed by NRIs inheriting Indian property.
Form 67
Filed with Indian ITR to claim credit for US taxes paid. Required for DTAA benefits in India. Filed by your India CA.
Form 1116
IRS form to credit Indian taxes paid against US liability. Dollar-for-dollar. Prevents double taxation. Your US CPA files this.
CAS  Consolidated Account Statement
All MF holdings across all AMCs in one statement. Get from camscollindia.com or kfintech.com using PAN + email. Essential before redeeming.
Tax & Capital Gains
TDS  Tax Deducted at Source
Tax withheld before income reaches you. 30% on NRO interest. Deducted by AMC on MF redemptions. Buyer deducts from property sale price. Reclaim excess via ITR.
LTCG  Long-Term Capital Gains
Equity/MF held >1 year: 12.5% on gains above ₹1.25L. Property held >2 years: 20% with indexation (12.5% without).
STCG  Short-Term Capital Gains
Equity <1 year: 15%. Property <2 years: 30%+ for NRIs. Property STCG TDS can exceed 35% of the full sale amount.
ITR  Income Tax Return
Annual India tax filing. Required when India income >₹2.5L (excluding NRE interest). Also how you reclaim excess TDS. Banks request it before large remittances.
DTAA  Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement
India–US treaty preventing full double taxation. Doesn’t eliminate tax — you pay in both, then offset via credits. India CA + US CPA must coordinate.
Foreign Tax Credit (FTC)
Reduces US federal tax by Indian taxes paid on the same income. Dollar-for-dollar on Form 1116. Without it, India income gets taxed twice.
Advance Tax
Quarterly pre-payment of Indian income tax when liability exceeds ₹10,000. Required after large property sales. 1%/month interest if unpaid.
Section 195(3)  Lower TDS Certificate
If actual capital gains tax < TDS rate, CA applies for a certificate allowing buyer to deduct at lower rate. File 30–60 days before sale.
Indexation
Adjusts purchase price for inflation to reduce taxable property gains. Uses the Cost Inflation Index (CII). Can significantly lower LTCG tax.
Investments & Retirement
Mutual Fund (MF)
Pooled investment managed by an AMC. Update KYC to NRI before redeeming. AMC deducts TDS before crediting NRO. Get cost basis from CAMS/KFintech first.
EPF  Employee Provident Fund
Employer retirement savings. Check all past employers via UAN on epfindia.gov.in. 5+ years service usually means cleaner tax — confirm with CA for NRI position.
PPF  Public Provident Fund
15-year government scheme, tax-free maturity. NRIs cannot make fresh contributions. Existing accounts held to maturity. Stop contributing immediately on becoming NRI.
NPS  National Pension System
At 60: 60% lump sum (tax-free), 40% annuity (ongoing Indian income). Premature: only 20% lump sum, 80% annuity. Factor annuity into cross-border tax planning.
LIC / ULIP / Endowment
Insurance policies. Maturity usually tax-free if premium <10% of sum assured. Compare surrender vs maturity — for policies in last 2–3 years, waiting often wins.
Demat Account
Electronic account holding shares. NRIs need NRI-designated demat linked to NRO/NRE. Some brokers require a fresh NRI demat. Check before selling.
FEMA, Legal & Residency
NRI  Non-Resident Indian
Under FEMA: <182 days in India in the FY. Determines repatriation rights — not your visa, citizenship, or OCI card.
FEMA  Foreign Exchange Management Act
Indian law governing all cross-border money movement. Sets $1M NRO limit, 2-property lifetime limit, PPF restrictions. Violations attract penalties.
RNOR  Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident
Transitional status on return to India (1–3 years). Foreign income generally not taxable. Planning window for Roth conversions and 401k withdrawals.
SWIFT Wire Transfer
Global banking network for international transfers. Only legally compliant method for India–US remittances. Keep every confirmation for 7 years.
SFOP  Streamlined Foreign Offshore Procedures
IRS amnesty for non-willful FBAR/income failures. Catch up on 3 years returns + 6 years FBAR with reduced penalty. Use with qualified US CPA before any IRS enforcement.
Power of Attorney (POA)
Authorises someone in India to sell property and manage accounts on your behalf. Without POA, property sale requires physical presence in India.
FX Spread
Difference between real rate and bank rate. On ₹1 Cr, 1% spread = ₹1 lakh in hidden fees. Compare rates before committing to a large transfer.
