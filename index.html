Account & Status Issues
These are FEMA violations or high-urgency gaps. Fix before initiating any transfers.
✓
❗ Still holding a resident savings account as NRI
Detected from Step 1. This is a FEMA violation. Convert to NRO immediately — tell your bank in writing you are an NRI.
❗ Have not informed all Indian banks of NRI status
Banks need written notification. Failure to notify is a FEMA violation even if unintentional. Required before any repatriation.
❗ Planning to remit NRO funds without Form 15CA/15CB
Banks will block the transfer. Form 15CB is a CA certificate (not self-filed). Form 15CA is your self-declaration on incometax.gov.in. Both required for most NRO remittances above ₹5L.
Tax & Compliance Gaps
These create silent penalties that compound. Check honestly.
❗ No cost basis tracked for MF or stocks
Without original purchase price, you cannot compute capital gains, file accurately, or claim the correct tax. Get purchase history from CAMS, KFintech, or your broker before selling anything.
❗ Indian ITR not filed for years with India income
Banks will ask for ITR acknowledgments before large repatriations. Missing filings = blocked transfers. File all pending ITRs before initiating transfers.
❗ Unpaid Indian taxes — advance tax, capital gains, or TDS shortfall
Outstanding tax dues can block repatriation and attract penalties of 1-1.5% per month. Your CA needs to check and clear all dues before remittance.
❗ FBAR (FinCEN 114) not filed in the US
Required if total foreign accounts exceeded $10,000 at any point in the year. Includes NRE, NRO, MF folios, demat. Penalty: $10,000 per violation (non-willful). Check every prior year.
❗ Form 8938 (FATCA) not filed for high-balance years
Required when foreign financial assets exceed $50K end of year (single) or $75K at any point. Filed with federal return. $10,000 penalty for failure. Your US CPA should check prior years.
Estate & Access Gaps
Silent risks — invisible until there is a crisis.
❗ No nominee on some or all accounts
Without nominees, funds get locked in succession proceedings. Add nominees to every account, MF folio, and demat. Takes 10 minutes per account.
❗ Spouse cannot access accounts or India property independently
If something happened tonight, could your spouse access every account and manage the repatriation alone? Power of Attorney for India + a shared document of all accounts is the fix.

🎯 India + US Tax Layer
The most misunderstood part. “Already taxed in India” does not mean “done.”
India side
· TDS may already be deducted at source
· Capital gains tax applies on sale
· Rental income taxable as India income
· File ITR to claim excess TDS refund
US side
· You report global income to IRS
· Claim Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116)
· Avoids double taxation via DTAA
· FBAR + FATCA reporting required
💡 Map each income type separately. Interest, capital gains, and rental income have different tax treatment in both countries. Your US CPA and India CA need to work together on the numbers — not in isolation.

Phase 1
Establish Your New Status
Legal & tax identity first — everything flows from this
5 steps
▾
💡 Why this comes first. Your ability to repatriate, your tax obligations, and which accounts you can hold all depend on your FEMA residential status. Establish this before doing anything else.
Understand Your Status
Confirm you are NRI under FEMA
Fewer than 182 days in India in the financial year. This determines repatriation rights — not your visa status.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
Update residential status with all Indian banks in writing
Inform each bank you are NRI. Required by FEMA. Failure is a violation even if unintentional. Do this before any transfers.
⚡ Do this first
Convert resident savings accounts to NRO immediately
Holding a resident savings account as an NRI is a FEMA violation. Convert every account — this is the single highest-urgency fix.
⚡ Do this first
Professional Setup
Hire a CA experienced in NRI + FEMA + 15CA/15CB
Not a general practice CA. Ask specifically for experience filing Form 15CA/15CB and working with NRI clients on remittances and ITR filing.
⚡ Do this first
Run a full inventory of every India financial account
NRE/NRO accounts, FDs, MF folios (CAMS CAS), demat, property, EPF (EPFO portal), PPF, LIC policies, NPS. You cannot move what you do not know exists.
☑️ One-time

Phase 2
Sort Your Bank Accounts
NRE = free. NRO = paperwork. Know the difference.
8 steps
▾
💡 NRE vs NRO is where most people get stuck. NRE funds are freely repatriable — no limit, no tax, wire directly. NRO funds require Form 15CA/15CB and proof of tax payment, limited to $1M per financial year.
NRE — the easy path
Identify all NRE savings accounts and FDs
These hold money that originated abroad. Interest is tax-free in India.
🏦 Bank action
Wire NRE funds directly to your US bank
No limit. No TDS. No Form 15CA. No CA certificate. Just a standard wire. Do this first — it is the cleanest path.
🏦 Bank action
Calculate premature closure penalty on NRE FDs before breaking
Typically 0.5–1% interest penalty. Calculate if waiting until maturity saves more.
☑️ One-time
NRO — the paperwork path
Understand the $1M annual NRO repatriation limit
FEMA: up to USD $1M from NRO per financial year (April–March) per person. A couple can move $2M/year combined. Spread large amounts across financial years.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
Compute income, pay any pending taxes
Your CA must verify all Indian taxes are paid before certifying 15CB. Outstanding dues block the remittance.
📞 Tax matter
Get Form 15CB from your CA
CA certifies taxes are paid on funds being repatriated. Cannot be self-filed. One certificate per remittance.
📞 Tax matter
File Form 15CA on incometax.gov.in
Your self-declaration before the remittance. Required for most remittances above ₹5L. File after 15CB is ready.
📞 Tax matter
Submit your bank’s A2 form + 15CA/15CB to process wire
Banks require their own A2 form (Purpose of Remittance) plus 15CA/15CB. Ask your specific bank for their current checklist before visiting.
🏦 Bank action
Collect Form 16A for all TDS deducted on NRO interest
30% TDS is auto-deducted on NRO interest. Form 16A lets you claim it as credit in both your Indian ITR and US FTC filing.
📞 Tax matter
Account Hygiene
Formally close dormant accounts you no longer need
Do not just stop using them. Formally close — dormant NRO accounts create ongoing compliance obligations.
☑️ One-time
Update nominees on all active accounts
Add nominees to every bank account, MF folio, and demat. Cross-border estate cleanup without nominees is a nightmare.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
📊
Phase 3
Liquidate Investments
MF, stocks, FDs, PPF — sell, pay tax, move to NRO, remit
9 steps
▾
💡 The flow for all investments is the same: Sell → TDS deducted at source → Proceeds hit NRO → 15CA/15CB → Wire to US. Know the TDS rate before you sell.
Mutual Funds
Get Consolidated Account Statement from CAMS or KFintech
Request CAS using your PAN and email. Shows every folio across all AMCs. Do this before anything else.
☑️ One-time
Collect original purchase history and cost basis
Without cost basis you cannot compute capital gains, file accurately, or claim the correct tax. Get from CAMS, KFintech, or your broker’s statement.
⚡ Do this first
Update KYC to NRI status with each AMC before redeeming
Resident KYC may block redemption. Update to NRI KYC first. Takes a few days per AMC.
☑️ One-time
Redeem MF — TDS deducted, proceeds hit NRO, then remit
Equity: 15% STCG (<1yr), 12.5% LTCG (>1yr on gains above ₹1.25L). Debt funds: 30% TDS flat for NRIs.
📞 Tax matter
Stocks & Demat
Ensure broker has NRI status updated before selling
Update demat/broker account to NRI status. Some brokers require a fresh NRI demat linked to NRE/NRO.
☑️ One-time
Sell stocks — proceeds to NRO — then remit
Equity: 15% STCG (<1yr), 12.5% LTCG (>1yr above ₹1.25L). Do not assume TDS certificates from broker will automatically flow to your ITR — check each one.
📞 Tax matter
PPF (Public Provident Fund)
Stop PPF contributions immediately if you have not already
Contributing to PPF after becoming NRI is a FEMA violation. The most commonly missed rule. Stop now.
⚡ Do this first
Hold PPF until 15-year maturity — then withdraw tax-free via NRO
NRIs can hold (not contribute to) existing PPF. Entire corpus is tax-free on maturity.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
File ITR for year of investment sale to claim any TDS refund
If TDS was deducted at 30% but actual liability is lower (especially for smaller gains), file ITR to claim refund to NRO account.
📞 Tax matter

Phase 4
Deal with Real Estate
TDS, capital gains, repatriation limits — the most complex piece
7 steps
▾
💡 Real estate is the hardest asset to move. High TDS, multiple approvals, lifetime limit of 2 residential properties. The buyer is legally required to deduct TDS from your sale amount — plan for it upfront.
Before You Sell
Gather all original purchase documents for cost basis
Sale deed, registration, builder agreement, payment receipts, home loan closure certificate. Needed for capital gains calculation and to potentially apply for lower TDS.
☑️ One-time
Brief the buyer: they must deduct TDS from sale price
LTCG (>2 years): ~23% effective TDS. STCG (<2 years): ~35% effective TDS. This is not optional for the buyer. Budget for it in your asking price.
⚡ Do this first
Apply for lower TDS certificate under Section 195(3)
If actual capital gains tax is lower than TDS rate, your CA can apply to Income Tax Dept for a certificate allowing buyer to deduct less. File early — takes 30–60 days.
📞 Tax matter
After Sale
Get Form 16A (TDS certificate) from buyer
Buyer must confirm TDS deposited with government. Use this to claim credit in your Indian ITR and potentially recover excess TDS.
📞 Tax matter
File ITR for year of sale — claim excess TDS refund
Capital gains must be declared even as NRI. If TDS exceeded actual tax liability, ITR filing triggers the refund to NRO.
📞 Tax matter
Know the lifetime limit: 2 residential properties max
FEMA allows repatriation of proceeds from up to 2 residential properties in a lifetime. No limit on commercial property.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
Submit 15CA/15CB with property docs — allow 2–4 weeks
Additional docs: sale deed copy, capital gains calculation, ITR acknowledgment, TDS certificate. Banks take longer for large property remittances.

Phase 5
Insurance & Retirement Funds
LIC, EPF, NPS, Gratuity — do not leave money behind
7 steps
▾
💡 These are often forgotten. EPF from a job you left in 2012. LIC your parents took in your name. A dormant NPS account. Run a full inventory first.
EPF
Withdraw EPF if not returning to Indian employment
Check EPFO portal for all past employer accounts via UAN. Inactive EPF still earns 8.25% but stops compounding after 36 months of no contribution.
☑️ One-time
Check service total: 5+ years = tax-free EPF withdrawal
Total service across all employers. If under 5 years, 10% TDS applies. File ITR to claim refund if actual liability is lower.
📞 Tax matter
Claim any pending gratuity from last Indian employer
5+ years service = entitled to gratuity. Up to ₹20L is tax-free. Contact HR of last employer if not paid.
☑️ One-time
NPS
Decide: premature NPS exit vs hold to 60
Premature exit: 80% must buy annuity in India (only 20% lump sum). At 60: 60% lump sum (tax-free), 40% mandatory annuity. Model both options with your CA.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
LIC & Insurance
Inventory all LIC, ULIP, endowment policies
Check surrender value vs maturity value. Sometimes waiting to maturity beats surrendering early, especially for policies in final 3 years.
☑️ One-time
Surrender LIC — proceeds credited to NRO, then remit
Tax-free under Section 10(10D) if premium <10% of sum assured. Stop premium payments on policies you plan to surrender.
🏦 Bank action
Stop premium payments on policies you plan to surrender
Do not keep paying from NRO if you are going to exit. Ask about the paid-up option — you stop paying but the policy stays alive at a reduced amount.
☑️ One-time
📋
Phase 6
India + US Tax Filings
Stay compliant on both sides — missing filings block transfers
6 steps
▾
💡 Both countries require action. India wants your ITR. The US wants your FBAR and FATCA. Missing either creates penalties and blocks remittances.
India ITR
File ITR for all years you had India income above ₹2.5L
Rental income, capital gains, NRO interest, dividends all count. NRE interest is exempt. File pending years before initiating large transfers.
📞 Tax matter
Claim DTAA relief — attach Form 67 with Indian ITR
India–US DTAA lets you credit Indian taxes against US tax liability. File Form 67 with your Indian ITR. Your CA and US CPA need to coordinate the numbers.
📞 Tax matter
Claim excess TDS refunds via ITR
30% TDS on NRO interest or MF debt redemptions may exceed actual liability. File ITR to recover overpaid tax to NRO account.
📞 Tax matter
US Filings
File FBAR (FinCEN 114) for all years with foreign accounts >$10K
Includes NRE, NRO, MF folios, demat. Deadline April 15 (auto-extended to Oct 15). Check every prior year — penalties are $10K+ per violation.
⚡ Do this first
File Form 8938 (FATCA) if foreign assets exceed $50K
Filed with federal return. $50K end-of-year or $75K at any point for single filers. Higher thresholds for MFJ. $10K penalty for failure.
⚡ Do this first
Claim Foreign Tax Credit on IRS Form 1116
Indian taxes paid (TDS on NRO interest, capital gains) reduce US tax dollar for dollar. Your US CPA files this. Prevents true double taxation.
📞 Tax matter

Phase 7
Execute the Transfers
Wire it home — the mechanics of moving money legally
5 steps
▾
💡 This is the payoff phase. Phases 1–6 done properly means this is straightforward. Most transfers get blocked because paperwork was not ready — not because the money is hard to move.
Execution Order
Build complete document packet before visiting the bank
Form 15CA + 15CB + source of funds proof (sale deed/MF statement/FD certificate) + PAN + passport + ITR acknowledgment + KYC. Incomplete submissions restart the clock.
☑️ One-time
Wire NRE funds first — no paperwork, no limit, no TDS
Always drain NRE first. Nothing to prepare, just initiate a SWIFT wire from NRE account to US bank.
🏦 Bank action
Spread NRO transfers across financial years if above $1M
$1M per person per April–March year. Spread large amounts across years or use both spouses’ $1M limits simultaneously.
⚖️ Legal / FEMA
Only use SWIFT wire transfers — zero exceptions
Hawala or informal channels are FEMA criminal violations. Use only official SWIFT from your Indian bank. Document every transfer with a SWIFT confirmation.
⚡ Do this first
After Transfer
Keep all records for 7 years + brief your US CPA on each transfer
SWIFT confirmations, 15CA/15CB copies, A2 forms, TDS certificates. Tell your CPA the source of each transfer — investment proceeds, rental, sale — they have different US tax treatment.
